Summary

I interviews weirdos. You can read my transcripts.

I like measuring weirdness. How long can you talk to a stranger? What weird thing have you wasted all your money on? How long can you sit still? I like finding how a person became weird.

Why Be Weird

I want to find how I'm weird. I want to make something better than anything I could buy. I want to sell it to people who think it's better than anything they could buy. I want to wake up with weird things I want to do each day.

I live in a market economy. I trade stuff I make for stuff I want. Otherwise, I get hungry.

I basically want the same houses, cars and food you want. But you won't build the house I live in or the car I drive. Big companies already make most of the stuff I want. For example, Ferrari makes better cars than either of us.

Big companies make normal stuff. I want to make something weird. I want to find weird experiences I can't just buy. I want to be weird.

How To Be Weird

America is full of weirdos. People spend tons of money on obsessions like monster trucks. Or they think up a business and convince millions of people to buy a product they never knew they needed.

It's not just money. Ultra-marathoning only costs as much as a pair of shoes.

I want to figure out how these people become weird. So I ask them.

Who am I?

I'm Calvin. I'm a real person. I'm shaky at yoga. I deadlift on Saturdays. My favorite place is the beach.

I live in Sanford, Florida. I'm a web developer at Seminole State College. I like building web sites.
