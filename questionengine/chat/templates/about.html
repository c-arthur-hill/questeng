What is the problem?

Most conversations are not fun. You want them to end quickly.

When a conversation is good you want it to keep going. By practicing long conversations, you learn to have good conversations.

Conversations turn bad for a few reasons. These can be fixed:

  • The speakers can't break the ice
  • Neither side is listening
  • The speakers have different backgrounds
  • The conversation turns judgemental

What is this site?

This is a research project into better conversations. A software interviewer asks you questions. You respond. Over time the sytem learns to ask better questions. You get more interested. The conversations get longer. The conversations get better.

My goal is to figure out which conversational approaches end up lasting the longest. Then I'll share them with you.

Why have longer conversations?

A relationship is a conversation that never ended. Most people don't know how to hold a conversation. Most relationships aren't fun.

Becoming an expert is the best way to advance professionally. An expert is someone who can talk for a long time about a subject.

Getting other people to like you requires talking to them for a long time. Unless you're rich and beautiful.

In long conversations, you figure out who you are and what you believe.

How can I have longer conversations?

Practice.

It can be hard to find people to talk to when you're bad at talking. Maybe if you practiced with a computer for a bit, you could figure out what works for you.

A big part of holding a conversation is asking good questions. This site asks good questions. Well, we try! You can learn by example.
